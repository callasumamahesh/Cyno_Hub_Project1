Making Of Brinjal Curry

Requried Ingredients

4-6 small to medium-sized brinjals (eggplants), washed and chopped into cubes, 2 tablespoons cooking oil, 1 teaspoon mustard seeds, 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 large onion, finely chopped, 2 tomatoes, chopped, 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste, 1 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1-2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste), 1 teaspoon coriander powder, 1 teaspoon cumin powder, 1/2 teaspoon garam masala, Salt to taste, Chopped cilantro (coriander leaves) for garnish

Steps for Making Brinjal Curry

  1. Prepare Brinjals: Wash the brinjals, remove the stem, and chop them into cubes. You can soak them in water to prevent browning.
  2. Sauté Mustard and Cumin Seeds: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
  3. Add Onions: Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  4. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Add Spices: Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add Brinjals: Add the chopped brinjals and coat them well with the spice mixture.
  8. Cook Covered: Cover the pan and let the brinjals cook on low to medium heat until they are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  9. Check for Doneness: The brinjals are done when they are soft and cooked through. You can test by inserting a knife or fork into a piece; it should go in easily.
  10. Garnish: Garnish with chopped cilantro before serving.
