Making Of Egg Curry

Requried Ingredients

4-6 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and halved, 2 large onions, finely chopped, 2 tomatoes, chopped, 1/4 cup cooking oil, 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 bay leaf, 1 cinnamon stick, 2-3 green cardamom pods, 2-3 cloves, 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste, 1 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1-2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste), 1 teaspoon ground coriander, 1 teaspoon garam masala, Salt to taste, Chopped cilantro (coriander leaves) for garnish

Steps for Making Egg Curry

  1. Prepare Hard-Boiled Eggs: Boil the eggs until they are hard-boiled, then peel and cut them in half. Set aside.
  2. Sauté Onions: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves. Sauté for a minute until the spices release their aroma.
  3. Add Onions: Add chopped onions and cook until they become golden brown.
  4. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Add Spices: Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, ground coriander, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  7. Add Water: If the mixture becomes too dry, you can add a little water to form a thick gravy.
  8. Add Eggs: Gently place the halved boiled eggs into the curry, making sure they are coated with the sauce.
  9. Simmer: Allow the curry to simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the eggs to absorb the spices.
  10. Garnish: Garnish with chopped cilantro before serving.
